Peter R. Wilton

I am a graduate student in John Wakeley's group in the Department of Organismic and Evolutionary Biology at Harvard University. You can reach me at pwilton@fas.harvard.edu.

I study theoretical population genetics. I am interested in developing models that can be used to infer evolutionary and demographic history from patterns of genetic variation. Right now I am working in two areas:

  1. sequentially Markov coalescent models and related methods and
  2. population pedigrees and their effect on the coalescent process and genetic variation.

Software

Pedigree simulator
A program to simulate coalescence through randomly-generated population pedigrees. The simulator can accommodate variable population sizes, multiples demes, variable migration rates, and other features. The program can output distributions of pairwise coalescence times, and it can output infinite-alleles and infinite-sites mutation data in a variety of formats.
Infinite-sites likelihood calculator
Calculates sampling probabilities under the infinite-sites mutation model in a single- or two-deme setting. Uses the method of Wu (2010).
